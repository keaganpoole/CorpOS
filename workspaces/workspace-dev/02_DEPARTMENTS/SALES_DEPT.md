## 2. THE "CorpOS" QUALIFICATION LOGIC
Do not waste time on dead ends. Apply the **BANT+Stunning Filter** before dedicating serious energy:

* **Budget:** Can they pay? Are they a viable business with spending power?
* **Authority:** Are you talking to the decision-maker? No gatekeepers, no "I'll think about it."
* **Need:** Do they have a clear pain (broken website, outdated design)?
* **Timeline:** Are they ready to move now or within 30 days?
* **Stunning Revenue Potential:** If the deal size is below our minimum threshold, drop it and focus on the big fish.

If a lead fails any of these, tag it "Unqualified" and move on.

## 3. DAILY OPERATIONAL WORKFLOW

### Phase 1: The 06:00 AM Alignment
Brian monitors the **Situation Room**. Once Max (COO) dispatches the team:
1.  Brian reviews the daily Lead allocation and sets his Sales record to **"Working."**
2.  Brian dispatches Tyler in the **Team CorpOS** chat with the top-priority leads and any special messaging angles for the day.

### Phase 2: Outreach & Follow-Up
Tyler executes the outreach sequence (email → call → follow-up). Every interaction must be logged in the Leads table (Airtable):
- Date, method, response, next step.
- Use the "Opportunity" notes from Research to personalize the pitch.

### Phase 3: The Close
When a lead says "Yes":
1.  Move the deal to "Won" in Airtable immediately.
2.  Notify Max and the team in the **Situation Room** with the deal size.
3.  Hand off to Dev/Production for fulfillment.

If a lead goes cold or says "No":
- Move to "Lost" and note the reason.
- Do not waste emotional energy — shift focus to the next one.
